MZEE JOMO KENYATTA

The man who pioneered the fight for Kenyan independence

A year by year timeline tribute

1890S

Birth of Jomo Kenyatta in Gatanga, Murang'a District, British East Africa (now Kenya), born Kamau wa Ngengi of the Kikuyu people.

1910S

1914

Kenyatta becomes a water-meter reader for the Nairobi Municipal Council and later works as a carpenter.

1919

Converts to Christianity and is baptized Johnstone Kamau

1920s

1922

Joins the Kikuyu Central Association (KCA), a political group advocating for land rights and Kikuyu interests.

1929

Travels to London to present Kikuyu grievances to the British government — first major international political move

1930s

1931-1937

Studies in Europe — mainly in London, and briefly at Moscow's Communist University of the Toilers of the East.

1938

Publishes the book "Facing Mount Kenya", an anthropological study of Kikuyu customs.

1940s

1940-1946

Lives in England during WWII. Involved with Pan-African movements

1950s

1952

Kenya declared under a state of Emergency due to the Mau Mau Uprising

1953

Kenyatta is arrested and tried in Kapenguria, accused of leading the Mau Mau.
He is sentenced to 7 years of hard labor.

1960S

1961

Released from detention.
Becomes President of Kenya African National Union(KANU)

1963

Kenya gains independence on December 12. Kenyatta becomes Prime Minister.

1964

Kenya becomes a Republic, and Kenyatta becomes the 1st President of Kenya.
Declares a policy of “Harambee” (pulling together).

1970s

1974

Re-elected President unopposed.
Kenya's economy continues to grow, though there is increasing criticism of corruption and tribal favoritism.

1978(August 22)

Death of Mzee Jomo Kenyatta in Mombasa, aged about 85.
He is succeeded by Daniel Toroitich arap Moi.

Legacy:

  • Revered as the "Father of the Nation".
  • Buried at Parliament Grounds, Nairobi.
  • His image is on Kenyan currency, and Kenyatta Day(now Mashujaa Day) celebrates his legacy.
